# Voila! Tester Limitations

## Current status

Voila! is still in public beta / release-candidate stage.

It should be tested as an early product direction, not as a finished commercial product.

## Important limitation

Generated content must be reviewed by a human.

Voila! can help produce structured learning and training materials, but it should not be treated as the only source of truth.

## Input limitations

Output quality depends on the PDF.

Voila! works best with:

- clear text PDFs
- structured documents
- moderate page count
- simple layouts
- readable headings
- clean paragraphs

Voila! may struggle with:

- scanned documents
- image-heavy PDFs
- complex tables
- forms
- signatures
- multi-column layouts
- very large PDFs
- low-quality OCR
- documents with mixed languages
- documents with unusual formatting

## Sensitive documents

Do not use sensitive documents for early testing.

Avoid:

- personal data
- confidential company documents
- legal records
- medical records
- financial records
- safety-critical documents
- compliance-critical documents
- private contracts
- documents you are not allowed to share or process

## Generated content limitations

Generated outputs may contain:

- missing details
- incorrect summaries
- incomplete glossary terms
- weak quiz questions
- duplicated flashcards
- OCR mistakes
- formatting issues
- hallucinated or over-generalized content

Always review before use.

## Language limitations

Current language-pack RC path includes:

- English
- Romanian

Future candidates remain planned only:

- German
- Spanish
- Italian
- Portuguese

These should not be presented as completed language packs until they are implemented, reviewed and validated.

## Product limitations

Current project stage does not include:

- cloud accounts
- collaboration
- team management
- payment system
- enterprise workflows
- auto-update system
- polished commercial installer
- final licensing model

## Licensing limitation

No LICENSE file has been added yet.

The licensing and commercial model are still under evaluation.

Do not assume broad reuse rights until a license is explicitly published.

## Testing expectation

The goal of early testing is feedback.

Not perfection.

The most important questions are:

- Is the product direction useful?
- Is the target audience clear?
- Does the tester understand the value?
- Which output matters most?
- What blocks real usage?
- Would a supporter package make sense?
